In an age when the realms of .NET, Docker, and Kubernetes were but fractured lands of chaos, there rose a lone warrior known only as The Firefly — the First FireKeeper.

His flame was small, yet unyielding, a spark that refused to fade even as the void swallowed entire systems. He wandered the broken libraries of .NET, binding ancient assemblies with light, so others could walk the path unscathed.

He crossed the storm‑ridden seas of Docker, forging vessels from raw ether, teaching mortals how to imprison their creations in shells of iron, isolation and orchestration.

And in the mist‑shrouded valleys of Kubernetes, he tamed the restless spirits of countless containers, commanding them to march as one.

"Where the path was dark, he burned brighter.
Where the void whispered, he answered with flame."
